How race impacts academic success for African American students

Why it Matters

In our society, irrespective of geographic location, the racial achievement gap between people of black African descent and other races is wide, with black students more likely to receive lower grades, score lower on standardized tests, drop out of school, and be less likely to complete college and earn degrees, all of which can lead to socioeconomic consequences, low job opportunities, less room for social mobility, and less representation in certain fields such as politics. This is an important issue because we thoroughly believe every single student, no matter what race, should have the opportunity to reach the highest levels of academic success throughout their educational journey, whether it be from kindergarten all the way to the tertiary level of learning. By educating more people about this social issue, in addition to those who practice allyship, the achievement gap in the black community will close, resulting in a greater representation of employed people, independence, social mobility, and prosperity, as some factors that lead to this gap in academic success, such as socioeconomic factors, lack of funding, and racism, will be greatly reduced.

About

A theme that has emerged within the educational system is the lack of engagement between some students and instructors. In a study about the engagement between them, an African American student said "The teachers don't care". This creates bad dialogue between students and teachers and makes a bad learning environment. Many students of color also feel as if they would be treated differently if they were white, or if they appealed to their teachers. So why does this matter? With all the different issues facing Black students in our educational system it is hurting them for the rest of their lives. When students are discriminated against while trying to learn to be successful later in life they are being stripped of the ability to make a living for themselves in the future and face increased levels of poverty along with decreased job opportunities. Education is the gateway to success for the rest of your life and if students are receiving less opportunities while in school they will have less opportunities for the rest of their life. Educational opportunities should be available to all people despite their background, race or financial situation. Schooling and education is the foundation and building blocks for kids and their development. We need to do everything in our power to provide for the next generation and the only way to be able to achieve this is by providing opportunities for the people who were not given them to begin with.

The problem with the discipline in schools with kids of different ethnicities. Race and Revolution: How race and class are hurdles to educational success

In the video, statistics show that during students’ earlier years of school, the attainment level between white British and black African students is almost the same; additionally, when compared to black African schoolchildren on free school meals (lower income families), the statistics show that it is a bit below that of wealthier kids. One of the beliefs behind this phenomenon is the issue of students having low aspirations during school, which was heavily noted as a common factor affecting African American students after reading literature on this social problem. Parents expecting their children not to attend higher education after secondary education is another factor that contributes to the academic success gap between races. This problem, which we noted from reading scholarly articles, happens due to black African American families tending to live in lower socioeconomic environments and areas, which has always been a problem since the abolishment of slavery. This means it’s hard for families to afford university and all the expenses that come along with it, leading to a repeated cycle of low attainment that is severely dangerous for future generations of students. Furthermore, during later years of schooling, it is noted that during A levels, a form of standardized testing in the United Kingdom, white students from all backgrounds overtake students of black origin, and at university, the difference widens with white students obtaining better degrees and being highly sustained in employment or further education, which directly correlates to earning. Furthermore, the video makes the point that the higher one goes up the educational ladder, the more racist it becomes, which is one of our arguments as well. Issues of slurs are usually ignored in schools, which can affect a student's mental health, leading to a student's inability to focus on schoolwork. In conclusion, barriers based on ethnicity and poverty exist that can make it difficult for students, especially those of black descent, to achieve the highest level of academic and educational success possible.

Structural Racism at a Glance

This image provides insight into how structural racism affects education, and how a corrupt education can impact the rest of someone's life. The image shows a cycle that shows us how having a negative schooling experience, due to structural racism, goes on to affect employment, then income, then housing. This is a perfect representation of how race impacts education, causing unequal education opportunities, which causes a lack of black students at universities, affecting everything else in the future. Additionally, an organization that is dedicated to changing racism in education is the Center for Racial Justice in Education. Their mission is educating educators and train them to be able to stop racism and discrimination in education. In order to increase academic success for African American students, the CRJE trains educators, parents, administrators and more in how to stop racial injustice in schools and the community. Visit https://centerracialjustice.org/ for more information.
