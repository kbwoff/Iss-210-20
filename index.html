How race impacts academic success for African American students

In our society, irrespective of geographic location, the racial achievement gap between people of black African descent and other races is wide, with black students more likely to receive lower grades, score lower on standardized tests, drop out of school, and be less likely to complete college and earn degrees, all of which can lead to socioeconomic consequences, low job opportunities, less room for social mobility, and less representation in certain fields such as politics. This is an important issue because we thoroughly believe every single student, no matter what race, should have the opportunity to reach the highest levels of academic success throughout their educational journey, whether it be from kindergarten all the way to the tertiary level of learning. By educating more people about this social issue, in addition to those who practice allyship, the achievement gap in the black community will close, resulting in a greater representation of employed people, independence, social mobility, and prosperity, as some factors that lead to this gap in academic success, such as socioeconomic factors, lack of funding, and racism, will be greatly reduced.
